Co-Founder and CEO of Microsoft Corporation

Bill Gates

William Henry Gates III is an American businessman, investor, philanthropist, and writer best known for co-founding the software company Microsoft with his childhood friend Paul Allen.

Bill Gates, born on October 28, 1955, in Seattle, Washington, is a co-founder of Microsoft Corporation,
a global technology company. He attended Harvard University but left before graduating to pursue his vision
of a personal computer for every home. Alongside Paul Allen, he established Microsoft in 1975, which
revolutionized the tech industry with its software products, particularly the Windows operating system.

Gates served as Microsoft's CEO until 2000 and remained active in its management until stepping down as chairman
in 2014. Beyond his work in technology, Gates is a prominent philanthropist. In 2000, he and his then-wife, Melinda,
founded the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, one of the world's largest private charitable organizations. The foundation
focuses on global health, education, and poverty alleviation.

Throughout his career, Gates has been recognized as one of the world's wealthiest individuals. His contributions to
technology and philanthropy have earned him numerous accolades and an enduring legacy in both fields.
